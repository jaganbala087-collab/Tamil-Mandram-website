The poet who travels with us

Valluvar — author of the Thirukkural — stands 133 feet tall at Kanyakumari, where three seas meet. Wherever Tamil goes, his words go too. We carry that spirit to the foothills of the Shivaliks.

தெய்வத்தான் ஆகா தெனினும் முயற்சிதன்
மெய்வருத்தக் கூலி தரும்.

"Even what divine fate would deny, the honest toil of the body that strives for it will surely repay."

Meaning: A task may seem beyond reach, even by fate or providence — yet sincere effort and hard work never go unrewarded; they pay the wages of the labour you put in.

— Thirukkural, Kural 619 · Chapter 62, ஆள்வினையுடைமை (Manly Effort) · Thiruvalluvar

About the Mandram

Tamil, far from home

The IIT Ropar Tamil Mandram is the campus community for everyone who speaks, studies or simply loves Tamil. Hundreds of kilometres from Tamil Nadu, we keep the language and its festivals alive in Rupnagar — and make every Tamil student, scholar and staff member feel a little closer to home.

We have no political or religious affiliation and we are open to all. Whether you grew up reciting Thirukkural or are learning your first words of Tamil, there is a place for you here. We celebrate the festivals, sing the songs, screen the films, and most of all — we look out for one another.

What we do

From the harvest of Pongal to the verses of a poetry night, the Mandram's calendar follows the Tamil year — adapted to a Punjab winter.

January · Thai

பொங்கல்Pongal Celebration

Our biggest day — the harvest festival, marked with the boiling-over pot, sugarcane, kolam at the threshold and a shared feast on campus.

April · Chithirai

தமிழ்ப் புத்தாண்டுTamil New Year

Puthandu vazhthukkal! We ring in the Tamil new year with mango, neem and a fresh start for the Mandram's year.

Autumn semester

வரவேற்புVanakkam — Freshers' Welcome

Every new batch of Tamil students gets a warm welcome, a buddy, and an instant circle of friends from day one.

Once a month

திரைப்படம்Tamil Movie Night

Classics and new releases on the big screen — the easiest excuse to gather, with popcorn and plenty of opinions afterwards.

Cultural fest

மன்ற இரவுMandram Night

Our showcase of Tamil music, classical and folk dance, skits and spoken word — performed by the community, for the whole campus.

Ongoing

Casual Tamil-learning meetups, Thirukkural readings and poetry evenings for fluent speakers and curious beginners alike.

Our flagship festival

பொங்கல்  Pongal, year by year

Pongal — the Tamil harvest festival — is the heart of our calendar: the boiling-over pot, the kolam at the doorstep, sugarcane, and a feast shared by all. Here is our Pongal, one year at a time. Add each year's photos as the album grows.

சிறப்பு · Why Tamil

The glory of Tamil

Tamil is one of the world's oldest living languages, with a literary tradition more than two thousand years old — from the Sangam poems to the Thirukkural. Recognised as a classical language and spoken by tens of millions today, it carries a heritage of poetry, Bharatanatyam, Carnatic music, towering temples and a celebrated cinema — a culture we carry with us wherever we go.

How to reach IIT Ropar

We're on the IIT Ropar main campus in Rupnagar, Punjab — on the banks of the Sutlej, about 45 km from Chandigarh.

By air — Chandigarh International Airport (IXC), about 60 km away (1.5–2 hours by taxi or bus).
By train — Rupnagar / Ropar Junction (RPAR), about 2.5 km from campus, with direct trains from Delhi, Ambala and Chandigarh.
By road — roughly 45 km from Chandigarh; regular buses run from Chandigarh ISBT, Sector 43, plus taxis and shared cabs.
